Permissions precedence cheat sheet

How people get burned across RBAC, SMB/NTFS, and SharePoint sharing — and how to avoid it.
Sanitized note: repeatable runbook format. No tenant IDs, secrets, internal hostnames, customer identifiers.

RBAC vs Sharing vs NTFS/Share (don’t mix the models)

  • RBAC = what admins can do in the control plane (Entra/Intune/M365 roles).
  • SMB Share permissions = a network gate, combined with NTFS for effective access.
  • NTFS ACLs = file-level control; inheritance/nesting/deny create “mystery access.”
  • SharePoint sharing = a grant path that can bypass your clean group model if unmanaged.

SMB: what supersedes what?

Over the network, effective permissions are the most restrictive combination of Share + NTFS. Share does not override NTFS — it can only further restrict.

SharePoint: why it feels different

  • No separate “share permission” layer like SMB.
  • Sharing links commonly add access through unique permissions/link grants.
  • Control sprawl by making group-based access the default and limiting ad-hoc sharing where it matters.
